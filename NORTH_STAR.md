# PenPal4ever — North Star

## What it is

A visual correspondence between two creatives. Martin and Daniel each respond to a shared theme — not with words, but with an image. The site is the exchange itself.

## The premise

Every theme is a prompt without being a prompt. It arrives as a title — spare, open, resistant to a single answer. Both penpals interpret it independently, then the site reveals both responses side by side, no explanation, no scores.

## The experience

The site should feel like opening a letter. There is anticipation (the intro), then revelation (the barn doors). The typography carries weight because the words *are* the theme — no decoration, no softening. Swiss precision in service of something human.

## What it is not

- Not a portfolio
- Not a social platform
- Not about likes or feedback
- Not trying to be more than it is

## Design principles

**Restraint.** One thing at a time. The theme first, then the responses. Nothing competes.

**Consistency.** The label and title look identical whether you're seeing the theme for the first time or navigating between them. The site doesn't shout when it changes.

**Motion with purpose.** The intro fly-up and barn door reveal aren't decoration — they mark transitions between states of knowing (not yet seen → seeing → comparing).


## User journey

**Starting a correspondence**
A user clicks NEW PENPAL and a modal asks for four things: their first name, their email, their penpal's name, their penpal's email. Submitting creates a new correspondence with a permanent URL — a shared address both parties can return to at any time.

**The rhythm of exchange**
Both users are presented with the same theme. Neither can move forward until both have uploaded their image for the current theme. When both have submitted, the next theme unlocks. This isn't a limitation — it's the structure that makes it a correspondence rather than a gallery. Each theme requires both voices before it closes.

**Looking back, not forward**
Users can navigate back through all completed themes at any time. Forward navigation only becomes available once both parties have responded to the most recent theme. The archive is always open; the future is earned.

**Ending a correspondence**
Either user can end the correspondence at any time. When they do, the exchange closes and a collection of all themes — both responses side by side — becomes available as a permanent record of the conversation.

**Multiple correspondences**
A user can participate in several correspondences simultaneously. Each has its own URL, its own timeline, its own penpal. The themes are shared across all of them, but every exchange is its own thread.

## Tonality reference

The following copy was written for the homepage and should serve as a reference for the voice and tone of the project — understated, precise, warm without being sentimental:

> A visual conversation between two friends, built around a shared theme.
> A new theme arrives when both of you have responded to the last one. You respond with an image. So do they. No explanations, no scores, no feed to scroll past it.
> Just a structure for staying creative with someone you care about, one image at a time.

## The long game

The archive grows one theme at a time. Eventually the full list becomes its own thing — a record of how two people see differently, theme after theme, over years.
